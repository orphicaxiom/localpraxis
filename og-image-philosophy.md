# Coastal Letterpress

A design philosophy rooted in the tension between permanence and place. Work that looks like it was made to last — pressed into material, not printed on demand. The visual language of craft trades: the business card left on a dock, the sign painted above a door that's been there thirty years.

Space is not empty — it breathes. Vast cream fields hold a few essential marks the way a bay holds light at dusk. Every element is placed with the deliberateness of someone who knows they have one sheet of paper. Composition is asymmetric only in the way nature is asymmetric: there is an underlying order, but it was arrived at through feeling, not formula.

Color is reduced to near-nothing: warm whites and inked darks with a single ember of terracotta that carries all the heat. That accent appears once, as a rule or a line — never decorative, always structural. It is the wax seal. The brand. The mark that says this came from somewhere specific.

Typography is the primary material. A serif logotype carries the weight of a century of craft tradition — meticulous, upright, unhurried. Beneath it, italic type leans into the warmth. Sans-serif appears only in whispers: small, spaced, subservient. The hierarchy is not designed; it is felt.

The result should appear as though someone labored over it for hours with a compositing stick and a proof press — each letter set by hand, each rule scored with a blade. Not digital. Not templated. Made.
