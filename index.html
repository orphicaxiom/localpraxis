Common questions.

Straight answers about how this works, what it costs, and what you actually get.

How long does a project take to build?

Most projects take 4 to 8 weeks from kickoff to launch. A focused booking system or client portal sits closer to 4 weeks. Something with inventory tracking, reporting, and staff roles might run 6 to 8 weeks. Before I write a line of code, we spend a week mapping out exactly how your business works — that upfront time is what keeps the build from dragging on.

I'll give you a realistic timeline in writing before we start, and I stick to it.

What does the monthly fee cover?

The $150–$400/month fee covers hosting, ongoing maintenance, security updates, backups, and routine changes to the software as your needs evolve. If something breaks — for any reason — I fix it, no extra charge. It also means you have a direct line to me when questions come up.

The rate depends on the complexity of what was built. We'll agree on a number before the project starts — no surprises.

What if I decide to stop paying — do I lose my software?

No. The code is yours outright. You own it from day one. If you ever stop the monthly fee, you keep everything — the source code, the database, all of it. You can take it to any developer in the world to host or maintain.

The monthly fee is for the ongoing service, not a subscription that locks you out. I have no interest in holding anyone hostage to a recurring bill.

Do I need any technical knowledge to use it?

None. That's the whole point — the software is designed around the way you actually work, not around how a generic platform assumes you work. If you can use email, you can use what I build.

Before launch I'll walk you through everything in person or over a screen share, and put together a simple reference guide tailored to your setup.

Will my employees be able to learn it?

Yes — usually faster than learning something off-the-shelf. Because the software matches your existing workflow, there's no mental translation required. Staff aren't learning a foreign system; they're doing the same job they already know, just with better tools.

I can build in role-based access so employees only see what's relevant to their job, which keeps things simpler for everyone.

Who handles hosting?

I do — it's included in the monthly fee. You don't need to set up an account anywhere, manage a server, or deal with a hosting company. I handle infrastructure, uptime, SSL, and backups. Your job is to run your business.

What if I need changes or new features later?

Routine changes — tweaking a form, adjusting a workflow, adding a field — are covered under the monthly fee. Most of these I can turn around within a few days.

Larger additions, like a completely new module or a major integration, are scoped and quoted separately. I'll always tell you upfront what's routine and what isn't — no ambiguity.

How is this different from Mindbody, Square, or Jobber?

Those platforms are built for the average business in your industry — which means they fit no one perfectly. You end up working around the software rather than the software working for you. And you're paying forever for a tool you'll never own, with features you'll never use crowding out the ones you need.

What I build is shaped entirely around your operation. Your terminology, your pricing, your workflow. No feature bloat, no per-seat pricing that scales against you, no vendor who can change the terms next year. After the build, costs are fixed and predictable — and the software is yours.
